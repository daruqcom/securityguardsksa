Security Guard Services for Oxagon

Oxagon's floating-city architecture is genuinely new. The security discipline it needs isn't. It's the same HCIS-aligned industrial and port guarding standard we've built over years at Jubail and Yanbu, applied to a coastal and floating model rather than reinvented from zero.

A New Shape, a Proven Playbook

Oxagon is planned as a floating industrial and port city on the Red Sea, architecturally unlike anything else in the Kingdom. But the underlying security requirements of an industrial and manufacturing hub haven't changed just because the platform is new: access control tied to permit-to-work systems, material reconciliation at gates, coordination with the site's own HSE and emergency-response teams. We bring the same discipline we've applied for years at Jubail and Yanbu rather than treating Oxagon's novelty as an excuse to improvise from scratch.

Where Land Security Meets Marine Security

Oxagon's coastal and floating-platform model means security genuinely spans both land-based and marine functions at once. Vessel and cargo coordination, gate procedures built for the rhythm of maritime-linked industrial logistics rather than a purely landlocked facility, and a single unified security relationship instead of separate land and marine contracts. This is the same integration we apply at Yanbu's export terminals, extended to a platform that adds a floating dimension most facilities never need to plan for.

Customs and Port Authority Coordination

Goods moving through Oxagon's port and manufacturing functions pass through customs and port-authority processes, and our gate procedures are built to interface with these cleanly, documentation checks that support customs clearance rather than duplicating or conflicting with it, so security doesn't become a second, redundant checkpoint slowing down legitimate cargo movement.

Advanced Manufacturing Brings Its Own Security Questions

As Oxagon develops toward automated and advanced manufacturing, security here starts to look less like pure physical-asset protection and more like protecting genuinely valuable robotics infrastructure and proprietary processes alongside the building itself, a layer most conventional industrial-security contracts in the Kingdom don't yet need to think about, but one we're building capacity for now.

A Multinational Workforce Needs More Than Bilingual Signage

Oxagon's construction and eventual operational workforce draws genuinely international engineering and manufacturing talent, and our guards here are briefed on functional English communication and basic cultural-awareness practice, not just handed a translated post order, real communication with a diverse workforce takes more than matching vocabulary.

One Relationship as the Site Moves From Construction to Operation

We think about Oxagon as a single long-term relationship spanning its full development arc, construction-focused staffing today, shifting toward full operator-grade industrial security as manufacturing and port functions genuinely come online, with our own HCIS-aligned training investment scaling to match each stage rather than requiring a fresh contract negotiation every time the site's activity changes.

Confined Spaces and Hazardous Material Storage

Industrial and manufacturing operations at this scale involve confined spaces and, in some facilities, hazardous material storage that need permit-to-enter enforcement beyond general site access. Our gate and patrol staff verify valid, current permits before anyone enters a restricted zone and log entry and exit separately from general site traffic, so a headcount inside a confined space is always immediately answerable during an emergency.

Coordinating With Marine Search and Rescue

A coastal, floating industrial platform carries genuine marine-emergency considerations that a landlocked facility never needs to plan for. Our security teams maintain clear coordination protocols with marine search-and-rescue and coast guard functions, so a man-overboard or vessel-emergency situation reaches the right specialized responder immediately rather than being treated as a standard on-land security incident.

Permit-to-Work Discipline in Confined and High-Risk Zones

Industrial facilities at Oxagon's scale include confined spaces and other high-risk zones requiring a permit-to-enter system beyond general site access control, and our security role enforces the boundary of that system directly: verifying anyone entering a confined-space zone holds a valid, current permit, and logging entry and exit times distinctly from the general site access log so a headcount inside a confined space is always immediately answerable during an emergency. This is the same discipline we apply at Jubail and Yanbu, and it doesn't get relaxed simply because Oxagon's architecture is new.

A Long-Term Industrial Security Partnership, Not a Single Contract

Oxagon will develop over years, moving from initial construction through manufacturing ramp-up to full port and industrial operation, and we structure our relationship here as a genuine long-term partnership that scales alongside each phase, construction-focused staffing today, transitioning toward full operator-grade industrial security as manufacturing and port functions come fully online, with our own investment in HCIS-aligned training growing to match the site's development at each stage rather than requiring a completely fresh vendor relationship every time Oxagon reaches its next milestone.

Floating Infrastructure Brings Its Own Structural-Security Considerations

Oxagon's floating-platform design means physical security planning includes structural-access points that don't exist at a conventional land-based industrial facility, mooring and platform-connection zones, for instance, need their own inspection routine alongside standard perimeter and gate security, since damage or tampering at a structural connection point carries consequences beyond a typical fence-line breach.

Coordinating Security Across Contractor Joint Ventures

Oxagon's construction involves multiple joint-venture partnerships between Saudi and international engineering firms, each often maintaining its own internal security liaison, and our role includes coordinating a single consistent site-wide standard across these partnerships rather than letting each joint venture run its own disconnected security arrangement within the same overall site boundary.

Supply Chain Security for Advanced Manufacturing Components

As Oxagon's manufacturing functions come online, high-value component supply chains moving through the facility need the same theft-prevention and inventory-verification discipline we apply at any advanced manufacturing site: material reconciliation at every gate crossing, and coordination with the facility's own quality-control systems on component tracking.

Coordinating Security Shift Patterns With Tidal and Marine Conditions

Given Oxagon's coastal and floating infrastructure, certain marine-security functions need to work around tidal patterns and sea conditions rather than a flat shift schedule, vessel-based patrol timing adjusted to when marine access is genuinely practical, coordinated with the same maritime scheduling the facility's own port operations already follow.

Requesting a Proposal Built for a Genuinely Novel Model

Send us your facility type, HCIS status, and role within Oxagon's floating industrial ecosystem, and we'll return a proposal that applies our decades of established Eastern Province industrial-security experience to this genuinely novel architectural model, not a proposal that treats the floating-city concept as requiring an entirely unproven, invented-from-scratch approach.

Our team has found that the fundamentals travel well even when the physical form is new: HCIS-aligned vetting, permit-to-work discipline, and documented roster math work the same way at Oxagon as they do at Jubail, adapted specifically to a coastal and floating-infrastructure context rather than reinvented from nothing.

A Final Thought on Building Something Genuinely New

Oxagon's ambition to redefine what an industrial city can look like resonates with how we think about our own role, applying decades of proven fundamentals with genuine creativity to circumstances that don't quite match any existing template. That's not a comfortable position for a security provider to work from, but it's exactly the kind of challenge worth taking seriously rather than avoiding.

One Relationship That Grows With the Site's Own Evolution

As Oxagon develops from initial construction toward its eventual role as a functioning floating industrial city, we see our security relationship here as a long-term partnership tracking each phase, construction-focused staffing today, transitioning toward full operator-grade industrial security as manufacturing and port operations come genuinely online, with our own investment in HCIS-aligned training and documentation growing to match the site's development at each successive stage rather than staying fixed at today's requirements.

Getting Started With Oxagon

Whatever stage your Oxagon-linked facility is at, reach out today and let's begin a conversation about exactly what your site needs, not a generic industrial-security proposal, but one built around your specific circumstances and current HCIS status.

A Final Word

Oxagon's ambition to redefine industrial infrastructure deserves a security partner equally committed to doing this work carefully, thoroughly, and well, from the very first gate we ever staffed there right through to today, and every day still to come as the site continues its transition toward full operation.
