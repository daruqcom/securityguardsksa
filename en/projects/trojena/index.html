Security Guard Services for Trojena

Trojena is a construction site today and a Winter Games host in 2029, and building genuine mountain-terrain and cold-weather security capability now, rather than scrambling for it later, is the whole point of how we've approached this project.

Two Projects in One, on the Same Site

Right now, Trojena is a mountain construction project, and the guarding it needs looks like any large Saudi construction site with one real difference: elevation, terrain, and weather that a flat-desert site never has to plan around. In a few years, the same site needs to run as an international winter-sports and tourism destination hosting the Asian Winter Games 2029: an entirely different security discipline built around hospitality, spectators, and athletes. We're building capability for both now, deliberately, rather than treating today's construction contract as disconnected from what the site becomes next.

What Mountain Terrain Actually Changes About Construction Security

Elevation changes patrol logistics in ways flat-site training doesn't prepare a guard for: routes that take three times longer on foot than the map suggests, vehicle access that a winter storm can close with a few hours' notice, and coordination with the project's own site-safety team on which access restrictions are currently active. We brief guards specifically on this terrain rather than assuming standard desert-construction training transfers cleanly.

Genuine Cold-Weather Capability, Not Borrowed Gear

Most Saudi security providers have never staffed a genuinely cold, snow-adjacent site, because almost nowhere else in the Kingdom is one. We've built real cold-weather operational capacity specifically for Trojena: appropriate guard equipment, training on how snow and ice change basic patrol safety, and a rotation schedule that accounts for the genuine physical toll of outdoor work in conditions most of our guards elsewhere in the Kingdom never encounter.

Snowmaking Infrastructure Is Critical Infrastructure

Trojena's artificial snowmaking system represents a serious engineering investment, and the water storage and pumping infrastructure behind it needs the same protection discipline we'd apply to any critical utility system. Because damage here doesn't just cost money, it can shut down the destination's entire operating premise for a season.

Getting Ready for 2029 Starts Years Before the Opening Ceremony

Hosting an Asian Winter Games brings international sporting-federation security and accreditation requirements on top of standard Saudi event regulation, a genuinely higher bar than any domestic event on our calendar. We're building the specific expertise this requires now: cold-weather event operations, international broadcast-security coordination, and accreditation processes that take real years to do properly rather than a crash course assembled the season before the Games arrive.

A Team That Moves With the Project, Not Against It

We've deliberately cross-trained our construction-security specialists here alongside our hospitality and event-security teams, so the eventual shift from construction guarding to full winter-tourism and Games-time operations happens as a continuation, with the same institutional knowledge of the site, rather than a wholesale team replacement the year everything changes.

Slope Safety Isn't Just a Ski-Patrol Job

Once Trojena is operational, avalanche-risk zones, chairlift and gondola access points, and off-piste boundary areas all need coordinated security attention alongside the destination's own ski-patrol and slope-safety teams: restricting unauthorized access to closed runs, and knowing which incidents belong to security and which need an immediate handoff to mountain-rescue specialists. We're building this coordination model now rather than waiting until the first season is already underway.

Staffing for a Genuine Off-Season, Not a Flat Year-Round Number

Mountain and ski destinations everywhere run a real seasonal cycle, a winter peak and a considerably quieter shoulder period, and Trojena's staffing plan should reflect that honestly rather than either overstaffing the quiet months or leaving the destination thin exactly when snow-season demand peaks. We build this seasonal flex into the contract from the start.

Coordinating With Contractors Across NEOM's Broader Ecosystem

Trojena doesn't operate in isolation from the rest of NEOM's construction and logistics ecosystem: many of the same contracting firms, supply chains, and workforce pools serve Trojena alongside Oxagon, The Line, and Sindalah, and our security planning benefits directly from the broader NEOM-wide relationships we maintain across those other sub-projects. A contractor verification issue flagged at one NEOM site is information worth having before that same contractor's personnel show up at Trojena's gate.

Getting Guest Safety Right Before the First Guests Arrive

The single biggest mistake a tourism destination can make is treating guest-facing security as something to figure out during the soft opening. We work with Trojena-linked hospitality operators well ahead of any opening date to build the guest-safety, lost-child, and emergency-evacuation procedures a mountain resort genuinely needs: accounting for the specific realities of elevation, weather-related access changes, and a guest population that may be unfamiliar with mountain environments in a way a typical Saudi resort guest wouldn't be.

Altitude and Access-Road Security in Winter Conditions

Trojena's mountain access roads can become genuinely difficult to navigate during winter weather events, and security planning here includes vehicle readiness, appropriate tires and equipment for the specific conditions, alongside coordination with the site's own road-clearing operations so guard rotation and emergency response aren't left stranded by weather that a lowland Saudi construction site would never need to plan around.

Visitor and Media Access During Pre-Opening Test Events

As Trojena moves toward 2029, the site is likely to host test events and preview visits well before full public opening, each needing the same kind of coordinated, credential-verified access control we apply to any major event: distinct from day-to-day construction security, and requiring advance coordination with whichever organization is running that specific test event.

Wildlife and Natural-Habitat Considerations in Mountain Construction

Trojena's mountain setting includes natural habitat areas that construction planning has had to work around, and security patrol routes respect the same environmental boundaries the project's own conservation planning has established, a genuine overlap between security discipline and environmental commitment similar to what we apply at Red Sea Global's coastal properties.

Guest Accommodation Security Ahead of the Winter Games

Athletes, officials, and spectators attending the eventual 2029 Winter Games will need accommodation security built to the same standard as any major international sporting event, credential-based access for athlete villages distinct from general guest accommodation, coordinated with whatever international sporting body oversees accreditation for the Games themselves.

A Team Genuinely Invested in Trojena's Long Timeline

Trojena's road to 2029 runs through years of construction before a single spectator arrives for the Winter Games, and we've deliberately built our relationship here as a multi-year investment, recruiting and training guards specifically for cold-weather operations well ahead of when that expertise becomes urgently necessary, rather than scrambling to build this capability only once the Games are imminent.

Reach out with your specific facility and construction phase, and we'll build a proposal reflecting both today's genuine needs and the trajectory this remarkable project is on toward its eventual, much larger, hosting responsibilities.

Requesting a Proposal for Trojena

Send us your specific facility and construction phase at Trojena, and we'll return a proposal that genuinely accounts for the mountain site's current status and its long trajectory toward the 2029 Asian Winter Games, reflecting a project we've committed to understanding at every stage rather than only once the international spotlight arrives.

From today's construction dust to the eventual roar of Winter Games crowds, we're building a security foundation meant to genuinely last through every remaining phase of Trojena's journey, together, one careful step at a time.

Building Genuine Expertise, Not Borrowed Gear

Cold-weather security operations aren't something most Saudi providers have any real experience with, and we've deliberately avoided the shortcut of simply issuing warmer jackets to a standard guard team and calling it cold-weather readiness. Our Trojena team trains specifically on mountain-terrain patrol logistics, cold-weather equipment maintenance, and the coordination protocols a site where weather can genuinely restrict access requires, capability we're building years before the 2029 Games make it urgently necessary, precisely so it isn't rushed.

One Careful Step at a Time Toward 2029

Trojena represents the Kingdom writing a genuinely new chapter in its own tourism story, and being part of the security foundation for that chapter, from today's construction work through the eventual Winter Games and beyond, is exactly the kind of long-term, meaningful engagement we look for in our giga-project relationships.
