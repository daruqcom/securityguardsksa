Which Businesses Are Legally Required to Have 24/7 Security Guards in Saudi Arabia?

If you run a hotel, bank, hospital, or large retail operation in Saudi Arabia, 24-hour security guarding stopped being optional in 2025: a Ministry of Interior amendment made it a direct legal requirement for specific business categories, and the businesses affected haven't all caught up yet.

The 2025 MOI Executive Regulation Amendment

The amendment names specific categories that must maintain round-the-clock security guarding: hotels, banks, hospitals, gold and jewelry stores, and large markets or shopping malls above a certain size threshold. For these categories, 24-hour coverage is a compliance requirement, not a risk-management choice left to the operator's discretion.

A second tier of businesses — private and international schools, event halls, medical centers, and venues under the General Entertainment Authority's licensing — face a related but distinct requirement: security guarding during operating/work hours, which is a lighter obligation than full 24-hour coverage but still mandatory, not optional.

What Counts as Compliance

Meeting this requirement means MOI-licensed guards physically present for the full required period, not a security camera system or an alarm monitoring service alone. Those are useful complements, but they don't satisfy the guarding requirement on their own.

For a hotel or bank, this typically means manned guarding at entry points plus periodic patrol coverage of the full premises. For a mall above the size threshold, it usually means guards stationed at multiple entrances and patrol coverage of common areas, not just one guard at the main door.

What Happens If You Don't Comply

Non-compliance with the mandatory guarding requirement carries real regulatory risk, not just a theoretical one: inspections can and do check for guard presence and licensing status, and businesses in the mandated categories should treat this as a compliance line item, not a nice-to-have.

Categories Under the Mandatory 24-Hour Guarding Requirement

The May 2025 amendment to the Ministry of Interior's executive regulations made round-the-clock guarding mandatory, not discretionary, for several specific categories of business in the Kingdom: hotels, banks, hospitals, gold and jewelry shops, and large markets and malls above a defined size threshold must maintain 24-hour physical guarding coverage.

Categories Requiring Working-Hours Guarding

A separate but related set of categories — private and international schools, event halls, medical centers, and licensed entertainment or exhibition-authority venues — are required to maintain guarding coverage during their working or operating hours, rather than the full 24-hour standard applied to the first category.

What '24-Hour Guarding' Actually Requires

The mandate requires physical guard presence around the clock, not CCTV monitoring alone and not an alarm-response arrangement as a substitute. A business relying purely on cameras and a monitoring center, without a licensed guard physically present, is not satisfying this specific requirement even if its overall security posture is otherwise reasonable.

Determining Whether the Mandate Applies to You

If your business operates a hotel, bank branch, hospital, gold or jewelry shop, or a market or mall above the regulation's size threshold, 24-hour guarding is mandatory rather than optional. If you're unsure whether your specific business falls under this requirement or the working-hours category, a site survey against the regulation's actual wording settles the question rather than guessing based on a general sense of your business type.

Consequences of Non-Compliance

Beyond the direct security risk of leaving a mandated facility unguarded, non-compliance carries regulatory exposure that can affect a business's licensing standing, insurance coverage, and, for publicly visible incidents, reputational consequences that extend well beyond whatever the guarding contract itself would have cost.

How the Mandate Interacts With Insurance Requirements

Insurance policies for qualifying businesses increasingly reference the 2025 guarding mandate directly, meaning non-compliance can affect not just regulatory standing but insurance coverage itself in the event of a claim. Businesses should confirm with their insurer that their current guarding arrangement satisfies both the regulatory mandate and any policy-specific security requirements, since these two obligations, while related, aren't automatically identical.

Planning for Facilities Approaching the Size Threshold

Businesses expanding toward the regulation's size threshold, a growing mall, an expanding market, should plan for the mandate proactively rather than waiting until they technically cross the threshold, since building compliant guarding into an expansion plan is considerably smoother than retrofitting it after the fact.

A Note on Multi-Location Businesses

Businesses operating multiple locations should check the mandate against each individual site rather than assuming a single company-wide answer: a retail chain might have some locations above the size threshold requiring 24-hour guarding and others below it requiring only working-hours coverage, and treating the whole chain uniformly risks either overspending at smaller locations or under-complying at larger ones.

How the Regulation Defines the Size Threshold for Malls and Markets

The specific size threshold that triggers mandatory 24-hour guarding for malls and markets is defined in the regulation's own technical criteria, and business owners shouldn't rely on a rough sense of 'this feels like a big mall' to determine applicability. A precise reading of the regulation's actual size definition, or a site survey conducted specifically against that definition, is the only reliable way to confirm whether a specific property falls above or below the threshold.

Planning Ahead of a Ministry Inspection

Businesses subject to this mandate should expect periodic Ministry inspections verifying compliance, and maintaining current documentation — guard licensing, shift logs, and evidence of continuous coverage — ready for review at any time avoids the scramble of assembling this evidence reactively when an inspector arrives unannounced.

How This Mandate Interacts With Franchise and Chain Operations

Franchise operators running multiple branded locations under a single corporate umbrella should confirm compliance is being managed consistently across every franchisee location, not just the corporate-owned flagships, a franchise brand's overall reputation can be damaged by a single non-compliant location, even if the parent company itself maintains rigorous standards elsewhere.

Practical Steps for Confirming Compliance Today

If you're uncertain whether your business currently satisfies this mandate, start with a straightforward internal audit: confirm your facility type against the regulation's named categories, check your current guarding arrangement against the 24-hour physical presence standard (not just camera coverage), and if any gap exists, address it proactively rather than waiting for a Ministry inspection to identify it for you.

The Bottom Line on This Mandate

This mandate isn't a suggestion or a best practice: for the named categories, it's a firm legal requirement with real regulatory, insurance, and reputational consequences for non-compliance. If your business falls into any of the categories discussed, treating 24-hour physical guarding as non-negotiable, and confirming your current arrangement genuinely satisfies it rather than approximates it, protects your business considerably more than any cost saved by cutting corners here.
